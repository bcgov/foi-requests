What is a record?

Records are any thing on which information is recorded or stored. They can be graphic, electronic, mechanical or any other means, and include books, documents, maps, drawings, letters, vouchers and papers.

How are records stored?

B.C. Government records are typically stored and organized by subject, not by name. It’s important to provide the subject you're requesting records about so that we can help you find the right records.

The Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act (FOIPPA) gives you the right to access records held by the B.C. government.

Anyone can request records. There are no restrictions on your age or where you live.

This service can help you determine if you should request records from government, and, when you’re ready, it can help you submit your application.
